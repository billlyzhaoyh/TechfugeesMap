Interventions to model across three different models

List of intervention strategies to be modelled

  • Better personal hygiene/protection for the camp residents

    Real-world implementation:

    • masks (each resident in Moria get handed two hand-sewn masks for personal protection
    • regular hand-washing (this is difficult in Moria due to the irregular supply of water to the camp but in other camps, local humanitarian organisations are working hard to provide regular handing washing with distancing mechanism e.g. placing water buckets two metres apart from each other to promote social distancing
    • disinfection of camp common area (this is done by Kitrinos who are investing some resources to disinfect places like toilets)

  • Building up hospital capacity

    Real-world implementation:

    We don't yet know how much care capacity the two to three medical organisations on the ground in Moria has to offer on COVID specifically given providing basic health care on a day to day basis pre-COVID was already hard. For example, MSF clinic only has two doctors serving the whole camp (a video recording from a MSF doctor on LinkedIn) and Joel was saying they are contemplating of building another clinic specifically for COVID. This is consistent with what has been implemented in other camps where there is a triage point manned by the volunteer usually that then sends people on to COVID clinic or normal clinic but with the over crowded condition in Moria compared to the situation with camps on mainland Greece, this triage set-up might be hard to implement (Billy to check with Joel the progress on this in Moria). Beyond Moria, we know that Save the Children who manages the biggest refugee camp in the world in Cox Bazaar was already building new hospital beds for COVID to 400-500 as an aim in April.

  • Sectoring the camp

    Real-world implementation:

    • Divide camps into smaller zones to reduce global contact. This is reported to us from NGO partners in IDP camps in Afghanistan where demarcation of land is taking place to install physical barriers to isolate off household and reduce gathering in certain areas (the exact implementation needs to be checked over. Billy can try to get more accurate information if needed)
    • Switch from global level activity to local level activity. In Moria this is done by breaking up the food line which from the pictures we have seen can promote the rapid spread of the virus quickly all over the camp environment. The food line has been split into 2-4 smaller lines (exact information needs to be confirmed with Joel's NGO friend on the ground) and people attend those 'local' lines according to their living location. There might be a danger where people switch the usage of the food lines depending which line they perceive as safer or quicker so this concern needs to be addressed too.

    Model implementation:

    Potential insights:

  • Isolate/quarantine/transfer offsite

    Real-world implementation:

    • Isolation centres: This is something that MSF was considering building in April (Billy to check if this has been built yet) where there is going to be some tents and structures outside of the camp which serves as isolation centres. In mainland Greece camps like Ritsona and Malaka, spare facilities are converted to isolation centres where families where one person has contracted COVID19 are currently being isolated. There the problem is they are not that far away from other residents and they are forced to seperate in a housing unit without toilet and water point so they still share with other healthy residents water points and toilets. We have heard reports elsewhere (Nigeria and Afghanistan) where people see isolation centres as detainment so there are reports of people breaking out of such camps and reports of people getting sick but afraid to see doctors because they don't want to be 'locked up' in isolation centres.
    • Quarantine centres: This is for people who are not showing symptoms but have come in contact with people of confirmed cases of COVID19 as a result of manual contact tracing. People are also expected to say 7-14 days here. This is also for people who have recently returned from outside of the camp.
    • Transferred to adequate hospital facilities: there are currently plans in place to transfer the critical cases of COVID19 to the respected national healthcare facilities outside of the camp but how the government will react to this is yet unknown (if refugees' needs come in conflict with country's resident needs)

  • Shielding:

    Real-world implementation:

    • The concept is to shield the 'high-risk population' people aged above 60 or with comorbidity that can expose them to the risk of virus (HIV, TB to name a couple). This is currently being trialed in Yemen with the aim to promote to 6/7 other places after this pilot. Local actors are skeptical of the feasibility of implementing this for example, gaining community buy-in might be hard in areas where refugees themselves own the property which they will not agree to move out for someone else to use.
    • The study paper is linked here and more info on this can be found here
    • There are reports from MSF and other front line organisations that have encouraged people to practice self shiedling where people above certain age or with certain comorbidity will now remain indoors and their food will be delivered to them by community volunteers (this might not be practical for the isoboxes in the middle of the camp but it might be possible to do in the Olive grooves...)
